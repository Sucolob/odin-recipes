Vegan Burger

Description

A vegan burger, where you use vegetal protein instead of animal protein, is an excellent alternative to the usual burger. We will be following my wife's recipe... sort of.

In this recipe we will be using lentils as "meat" for the burger. Lentils are very healthy grains with almost as much protein content as red meat while also being more healthy.

Also, our primary objective here is to make the patty, not the actual burger. That part is left to the reader's discretion. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 500 g of Lentils
  • 250 g of Leek
  • 1 Tomato
  • 2 branches of Coriander
  • 1 head of Garlic
  • 30 g of Wheat bran
  • Water as needed
  • Oil of preference
  • Sea salt (or Himalayan or Black or whatever salt you want)/Pepper/Spices to taste

Equipment

  • Food processor
  • Oven (optional)
  • Assorted recipients and utensils

Procedure

  1. Wash the lentils thoroughly to make sure you get rid of whatever contaminant they may have.

    2. NOTE: There are several ways in which you can pre-prepare the lentils. You can just wash them lightly, you can wash them really thoroughly, you can leave them in water for a whole day (to soften them) while also washing them (stagnated lentil water is gross), or you can also germinate (or sprout) them.

    Sprouting lentils is as simple as leaving them in water for 1-3 days with proper washes in between. Then you can leave them moist inside the recipient you are using, or inside a colander, for another day. At the end of that day you should see the lentils growing roots, that is what sprouting is.

    Sprouting makes lentils, and grains in general, more nutritious by neutralizing phytic acid and making their nutrients more bioavailable. Phytic acid is a component of plants and is both a nutrient and anti-nutrient, but is better, overall, to remove it.

  2. Chop the leek, tomato, coriander and garlic real good and mix them.

    NOTE: Here you can (if you want) put this mixture into a pan and fry it in a little oil. You can add spices to taste to this mixture, but I advise you to keep the spices for a later step.

  3. Put the lentils in the Food processor and process them until they become a paste. Make sure not to overdo it with the processor as we want the paste to still have some bits of lentils (for the crunch factor).
  4. Mix the processed lentils and the tomato mixture with your utensil of choice in the recipient you want. Make sure to obtain as homogeneous a mixture as you can.
